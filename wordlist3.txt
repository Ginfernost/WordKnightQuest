(astronaut) a person who travels in space
(barbecue) a meal or gathering at which meat, fish, or other food is cooked out of doors on a rack over an open fire or on a special appliance.
(carousel) a rotating machine with seats for children to ride on or amusement, usually in the form of a merry-go-round at a fair.
(dolphin) a small gregarious toothed whale that typically has a beaklike snout and a curved fin on the back.
(elephant) a very large plant-eating mammal with a trunk.
(fountain) a structure that sends water up into the air in a decorative way.
(giraffe) a large African mammal with a very long neck and forelegs, having a coat patterned with brown patches separated by lighter lines.
(hurricane) a storm with a violent wind, in particular a tropical cyclone in the Caribbean.
(igloo) a dome-shaped Eskimo house, typically built from blocks of solid snow.
(jaguar) a large, heavily built cat that has a yellowish-brown coat with black spots, found mainly in the dense forests of Central and South America.
(koala) a small bearlike arboreal Australian marsupial that has thick fur and feeds on eucalyptus leaves.
(lighthouse) a tower or other structure containing a beacon light to warn or guide ships at sea.
(marathon) a long-distance running race, strictly one of 26 miles and 385 yards.
(ninja) a person skilled in ninjutsu, a Japanese martial art involving stealth and agility.
(octopus) a sea creature with a soft body and eight long arms around its mouth.
(penguin) a flightless seabird of the southern hemisphere, with black upper parts and white underparts and wings developed into flippers for swimming underwater.
(quadrilateral) a four-sided figure.
(rhinoceros) a large, heavily built plant-eating mammal with one or two horns on the nose and thick folded skin, native to Africa and South Asia.
(sunflower) a tall North American plant of the daisy family, with very large golden-rayed flowers.
(telescope) an optical instrument designed to make distant objects appear nearer, containing an arrangement of lenses, or of curved mirrors and lenses, by which rays of light are collected and focused and the resulting image magnified.
(umbrella) a device consisting of a circular canopy of cloth on a folding metal frame supported by a central rod, used as protection against rain or sometimes sun.
(volcano) a mountain or hill, typically conical, having a crater or vent through which lava, rock fragments, hot vapor, and gas are or have been erupted from the earth's crust.
(whale) a very large marine mammal with a streamlined hairless body, a horizontal tail fin, and a blowhole on top of the head for breathing.
(xylophone) a musical instrument played by striking a row of wooden bars of graduated length with one or more small wooden or plastic mallets.
(yo-yo) a toy consisting of a flat spool wound with string that is spun down from and reeled up to the hand by motions of the wrist.
(zeppelin) a large German dirigible airship of the early 20th century, long and cylindrical in shape and with a rigid framework.
(balloon) a brightly colored rubber sac inflated with air and then sealed at the neck, used as a children's toy or a decoration.
(caterpillar) the larva of a butterfly or moth, having a segmented wormlike body with three pairs of true legs and several pairs of leglike appendages.
(dragonfly) a fast-flying long-bodied predatory insect with two pairs of large transparent wings that are spread out sideways at rest.
(eclipse) an obscuring of the light from one celestial body by the passage of another between it and the observer or between it and its source of illumination.
(flamingo) a large wading bird with mainly pink or scarlet plumage and long legs and neck.
(gazelle) a small to medium-sized antelope with a slender neck and curved horns, found in open country in Africa and Asia.
(hedgehog) a small nocturnal Old World mammal with a spiny coat and short legs, able to roll itself into a ball for defense.
(iceberg) a large floating mass of ice detached from a glacier or ice sheet and carried out to sea.
(jackal) a slender, long-legged wild dog that feeds mainly on carrion, game, and fruit and often hunts cooperatively, found in Africa and southern Asia.
(kangaroo) a large Australian marsupial with strong hind legs for hopping and a long tail, typically living in groups in open country and feeding on grasses.
(leopard) a large solitary cat that has a fawn or brown coat with black spots and usually hunts at night, widespread in the forests of Africa and southern Asia.
(mushroom) a fungal growth that typically takes the form of a domed cap on a stalk, often with gills on the underside of the cap, and is found mainly in forests and other damp environments.
(nightingale) a small plain brownish European songbird noted for its rich melodious song, typically given by the male during the breeding season.
(oceanographer) a person who studies the ocean.
(parrot) a bird, typically vividly colored, with a short down-curved hooked bill, grasping feet, and a raucous voice, found especially in the tropics and feeding on fruits and seeds.
